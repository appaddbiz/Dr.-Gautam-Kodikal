Hip Arthroscopy – Minimally Invasive Relief for Hip Pain

Hip arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to diagnose and treat problems inside the hip joint. Through small incisions, a camera (arthroscope) and specialized instruments are inserted to repair damage without the need for open surgery. Dr. Gautam Kodikal offers advanced arthroscopic treatment for hip conditions, allowing for faster recovery and reduced post-operative discomfort.

When is Hip Arthroscopy Recommended?

This procedure is ideal for patients experiencing persistent hip pain, stiffness, or limited mobility due to soft tissue or structural issues. Common conditions treated include:

  • Femoroacetabular Impingement (FAI): Abnormal contact between the hip bones

  • Labral Tears: Damage to the cartilage ring around the hip socket

  • Hip Synovitis: Inflammation of the joint lining causing pain and swelling

  • Loose Bodies: Floating fragments of bone or cartilage inside the joint

  • Snapping Hip Syndrome: Tendons rubbing over bones during movement

Benefits of Hip Arthroscopy

  • Smaller incisions and minimal tissue damage

  • Reduced pain and quicker recovery compared to open surgery

  • Lower risk of complications and infection

  • Improved joint function and mobility
