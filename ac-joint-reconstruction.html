AC Joint Reconstruction – Restore Stability & Shoulder Strength

AC (Acromioclavicular) Joint Reconstruction is a surgical procedure to restore the alignment and stability of the shoulder joint after an injury, particularly when the ligaments connecting the clavicle to the scapula are torn or stretched. Dr. Gautam Kodikal specializes in advanced techniques for AC joint repair and reconstruction, offering personalized care to athletes, trauma patients, and individuals suffering from chronic AC joint instability or separation.

When is AC Joint Reconstruction Needed?

Surgery is recommended when conservative management such as rest, physiotherapy, or bracing fails to relieve pain or restore joint function. Common indications include:

  • Grade III or Higher AC Joint Dislocations: Where complete ligament tears lead to joint instability

  • Chronic AC Joint Separation: Long-standing cases with persistent pain and shoulder weakness

  • Failed Non-Surgical Treatment: Continued dysfunction despite conservative therapies

  • High-Demand Individuals: Athletes or laborers needing full shoulder strength and function

Surgical Techniques for AC Joint Reconstruction

  • Ligament Reconstruction: Using grafts (autografts or allografts) to reconstruct torn coracoclavicular ligaments

  • Hook Plate Fixation: Temporary stabilization using metal plates for severe injuries

  • Suspensory Fixation Devices: Minimally invasive techniques using strong suture-button constructs

Benefits of AC Joint Reconstruction

  • Restores joint alignment and stability for improved shoulder strength

  • Reduces chronic pain and discomfort associated with joint instability

  • Enables return to sports and physical activity with confidence
